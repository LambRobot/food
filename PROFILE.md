# My Food Profile — allergies & preferences

Personal constraints to factor into recipe scoring, recommendations, the Menu Helper, and any future
work. *(Public repo — nothing sensitive here beyond food preferences.)*

## 🚫 Allergies / intolerances

- **Fresh mint — only when crushed / muddled / squeezed in.** A reaction kicks in when **fresh** mint is
  *crushed, muddled, or juiced into* a dish or drink — **mojitos are a no-go.** Mild but enough to have
  a bad time.
  - ✅ **Fine when it's whole-leaf contact or garnish** — I can just pluck a whole fresh leaf off a
    roll/salad/plate. So: *whole fresh leaf on top = OK; fresh mint blended/crushed in = avoid.*
  - ✅ **Dry mint is ok-ish in low quantity** — dried mint in a spice mix, and **peppermint tisane in a
    low quantity**, have been fine. (It's specifically *fresh crushed* mint that's the problem.)

- **Raw bell pepper — can't digest it.** Anything where **raw** bell pepper is present, especially as
  a main ingredient → **avoid (e.g. gazpacho).**
  - ✅ **Cooked bell pepper is fine** as long as it's *not the main ingredient*.

- **Spinach — cooked spinach is a real no-go** (same reaction level as crushed mint). Sautéed/creamed
  spinach, spinach pie / spanakopita, spinach cooked into a dish → **avoid.**
  - ✅ **Raw young / baby spinach is fine when it's part of a mix** (e.g. a salad blend).

## 👍 / 👎 Preferences

- **No caffeine.** **Decaf is fine.** **Trace stimulants are fine — but flag them** (e.g. theobromine
  in chocolate/cocoa is OK; just call it out when it's present). It's *caffeine* and meaningful
  stimulants I avoid.
  - ☕ **"No tea" is about the caffeine, not the ritual — I enjoy the tradition of brewed tea.** So
    **caffeine-free brewed teas/tisanes and decaf teas are welcome** (hibiscus, rooibos, decaf green, etc.).
    → See [`caffeine_free_drinks.md`](caffeine_free_drinks.md).

- **Shrimp — like it, but not shrimp-forward.** I enjoy shrimp *within* a dish (e.g. jambalaya) but
  take fewer of them; I avoid shrimp-*heavy* dishes and shrimp cocktail. **Don't discard shrimp
  recipes** — just note "go lighter on the shrimp."

- **Oysters & shelled mollusks (clams, mussels, scallops) — usually steer away.** I'll eat them
  occasionally for a specific reason, but by default I avoid them.

## 🧭 How to apply this

| Signal | Action |
|---|---|
| **Fresh** mint **crushed/muddled/juiced** in (mojito, mint sauce, mint pesto) | **Avoid / flag** |
| **Fresh** mint as a **whole-leaf garnish** | OK (I'll pluck it) |
| **Dry** mint (spice mix, peppermint tisane) in **low quantity** | OK |
| **Trace stimulant** (theobromine in chocolate/cocoa) | OK — but **flag it** |
| Caffeinated drinks (regular coffee, caffeinated tea, energy) | Avoid — **decaf / caffeine-free only** |
| **Caffeine-free brewed** tea/tisane (hibiscus, rooibos, decaf tea) | ✅ Welcome (enjoys the ritual) |
| **Raw** bell pepper, especially as a main (gazpacho, raw-pepper salad) | **Avoid / flag** |
| Cooked bell pepper, minor ingredient | OK |
| **Cooked spinach** (sautéed, creamed, spinach pie/spanakopita, cooked into a dish) | **Avoid / flag** |
| Raw **baby/young spinach** in a salad mix | OK |
| **Shrimp-heavy** dish (shrimp cocktail, shrimp scampi as the whole plate) | Soft-flag — "lighter on shrimp," don't remove |
| **Oyster / clam / mussel / scallop-forward** dish | Soft-avoid |

## 🗺️ Side-quests
- [x] **Find a caffeine-free, antioxidant-rich drink** to replace tea/coffee → **done, see
  [`caffeine_free_drinks.md`](caffeine_free_drinks.md).** Short version: lean on **decaf coffee** (already
  a favourite — keeps coffee's antioxidants & liver benefit), plus **brewed hibiscus, rooibos, decaf
  green tea** (the brewing ritual is a plus), and **chicory "coffee."** Unsweetened cocoa is fine too —
  just flag its trace theobromine. Skip sugary antioxidant *juices* (fatty liver).
