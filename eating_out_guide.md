# Eating Out — the "Least-Worst Option" Guide (fatty-liver first)

> **Purpose.** When you can't cook — fast food, an airport, a restaurant with a fixed menu — this is how to pick the **best of the available options**. It's tuned for **fatty liver (MASLD)** first (per `fatty_liver_diet_wiki.md`), then general Mediterranean/nutrition sense.
>
> It's two things at once: a **glanceable cheat-sheet** you can use standing at a counter, **and** the exact rubric I use when you send me a menu photo or name a place — I rank the least-worst options and tell you why.
>
> ⚕︎ Educational, not medical advice.

---

## The 30-second order of operations (do these in order — biggest liver wins first)

1. **Kill the sugary drink.** Water, sparkling water, unsweetened tea, or black coffee. Soda/juice/sweet tea/sweet coffee is the **#1 driver of liver fat** and the single easiest win — bigger than any food choice.
2. **Skip the alcohol** (or keep it truly minimal). The liver is already stressed.
3. **Pick the protein grilled / baked / roasted / steamed — never fried or breaded.** Grilled chicken beats crispy chicken; a burger patty beats a breaded fillet.
4. **Make half the plate vegetables.** Side salad (dressing on the side), grilled veg, or a veg-forward dish instead of fries.
5. **Cut the refined carb base.** Half the bun / half the rice / skip the bread basket / thin crust. Refined starch spikes blood sugar → liver fat.
6. **Right-size it.** Order the smaller size, split an entrée, or box half before you start.
7. **Dessert:** fruit if offered, otherwise share one or skip. It's the top of the pyramid.

If you only do #1 and #3, you've already made the biggest difference.

## Green flags (seek these) vs. Red flags (minimize)

**🟢 Seek:** grilled · baked · roasted · steamed · poached · fish & seafood · beans/lentils/chickpeas · vegetables & salads · whole grains · olive oil / vinaigrette · water / coffee / unsweetened tea · "fresh," "house salad," "grilled," "steamed."

**🔴 Minimize:** fried · breaded · "crispy" · tempura · **sugary drinks / soda / juice / sweet tea** · sweet-glazed sauces (**BBQ, teriyaki, sweet-and-sour, honey-glazed, general tso's**) · creamy/cheesy (alfredo, queso, "smothered," "loaded") · bacon/sausage/processed meats · big refined-carb bases (giant buns, white-rice mounds, pasta, garlic bread) · desserts & pastries · alcohol.

*Two words that are almost always red flags: **"crispy"** (= fried) and **"glazed/sticky"** (= sugar).*

## Quick picks by setting (the usual least-worst)

- **Fast-food burger place (McDonald's etc.):** a **grilled** chicken sandwich or a salad with grilled chicken + vinaigrette; if it must be a burger, the **smallest single patty**, no sugary drink, easy on mayo-y "special sauces." Ditch the soda and downsize the fries — those two moves matter more than the burger itself.
- **Pizza:** thin crust, **veg toppings**, 2 slices + a side salad. Avoid stuffed crust, extra cheese, meat-lovers.
- **Mexican:** **burrito bowl** — skip or halve the rice, **black/pinto beans**, fajita veg, grilled protein, **salsa/pico** (not queso, sour cream, big cheese). Skip sugary drinks & margaritas.
- **Chinese / Asian:** **steamed** over fried; **avoid sweet/sticky sauces** (General Tso's, sweet-and-sour, orange, teriyaki); edamame, steamed dumplings, stir-fried veg; go light on white rice (or brown if offered).
- **Italian:** grilled fish/chicken + vegetables, **tomato-based over cream/alfredo**, half the pasta, skip the garlic bread.
- **Indian:** **tandoori/grilled**, **dal (lentils)**, veg curries that are **tomato-based rather than cream** (choose over butter/korma/makhani); go easy on naan and rice.
- **Breakfast / café:** eggs + vegetables, whole-grain toast; **skip pastries, pancakes, syrup, and juice**; black coffee is a *plus* for the liver.
- **Sandwich shop:** whole-grain bread, lots of veg, grilled/lean protein, mustard/oil-vinegar not mayo; skinny it (no chips + soda combo).

## How I rank a menu you send me

Send a **photo of the menu** or just tell me the place. For each item I estimate its fatty-liver impact — **added sugar/fructose, alcohol, saturated fat, refined carbs, frying, and portion size** — reward the good signals (veg, fish, legumes, whole grains, grilling), and then:

1. Give you the **top 1–3 least-worst picks**, best first.
2. Say **why** each is better (and what drags it down).
3. Add **easy tweaks** — "get it grilled, dressing on the side, swap fries for salad, water instead of soda."
4. Be honest about **confidence** — chains have published nutrition (accurate); independent restaurants are estimates from the description (directional, not precise). Sometimes the honest answer is "they're all rough — here's the least-bad, and here's how to fix it."

The goal is never a perfect meal — it's the **best decision available in that moment.**
