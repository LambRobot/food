Lemongrass and Coconut Curry with Summer Vegetables

Prep Time: 30 min Cook Time: 1 min Servings: 4 to 6 servings Source: Bonappetit.com

Ingredients

1 tablespoon vegetable oil

2 tablespoons (or more) Thai curry paste (3)

5 scallions, thinly sliced, white and pale-green parts and dark-green parts divided

3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced

1 1-inch piece peeled ginger, thinly sliced

6 cups mixed ¼-inch-thick sliced vegetables (such as Japanese eggplant, long beans, summer squash, and bell peppers) kale/mushroom/butternut squash for example

2 lemongrass stalks, bottom third only, tough outer layer removed, bruised with the back of a knife or a rolling pin

2 cups (or more) chicken broth or vegetable stock

1 13½-ounce can light unsweetened coconut milk (2 cups)

2 skinless, boneless chicken breasts, cut into small cubes (optional)

2 sprigs basil plus leaves for garnish

1 tablespoon (or more) fish sauce (such as nam pla or nuoc nam; optional)

Kosher salt

Steamed rice (optional)

Lime wedges (optional)

Directions

Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat.
Cook onion
Add ginger and garlic
Add 2 Tbsp. curry paste and cook, stirring, until fragrant, about 1 minute.

Increase heat to medium; add vegetables and lemongrass. Cook, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are just beginning to soften, 5–10 minutes. Add broth and coconut milk (add additional broth if needed to cover vegetables) and bring to a simmer.

Add chicken (lightly sauteed), if desired, and basil sprigs; very gently simmer until chicken is cooked through, about 10 minutes. Add 1 Tbsp. fish sauce, if desired. Add 1 tsp rice vinegar

Thicken with 1 tbsp cornstarch if needed

Season to taste with salt or more fish sauce or curry paste for extra flavor and spice, if desired. Garnish with dark-green parts of scallions and basil leaves. Serve with rice and lime wedges for squeezing over, if desired.
