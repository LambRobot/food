Caldo Verde (Portuguese Potato and Kale Soup with Sausage)

★★★★★

Full Veggies, Good Für Guests

Cook Time: 45 mins Servings: 6 servings Source: seriouseats.com

Ingredients

(edited by tom)

2 tablespoons (30g) unsalted butter

1 medium yellow onion or large leek, finely diced (about 8 ounces; 225g)

3 medium cloves garlic, sliced (about 1 tablespoon; 15g)

Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper

About 6 tablespoons (90ml) extra-virgin olive oil

1 large russet potato, peeled, quartered, and cut into fine 1/4in or smaller slices.

4 medium Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled, quartered, and cut into 1in pieces.

6 cups (1.4L) homemade or store-bought low-sodium chicken stock (see note)

1 bunch curly or lacinato kale, tough stems removed, leaves finely chopped (about 3/4 pound; 340g)

12 ounces (340g) cooked linguiça or other garlicky pork sausage, cut into 1/4- to 1/2-inch slices (see note)

Minced fresh chives, for garnish (optional)

Directions

(Edited by tom)

Cut onions press garlic.

Heat olive oil in a large Dutch over medium-low. Add onion (or leek) and garlic, season with salt and pepper, and cook, stirring frequently, until softened but not browned, about 5-10 minutes, adding olive oil as necessary to keep the mixture loose and moist.

Peel potatoes. quarter the russet then cut in fine blades. Cut the golden potato in 1.5 in pieces. From tom experience this allow to cool both at same time and have russet falling appart while golden potato still semi firm.

Add potatoes, sausages whole, and stock and bring to a simmer, stirring occasionally.

Once boiling Add kale and continue to cook for 5-8 minutes. Eemove sausage, cut in slices and put thel back in with juice.

Cook until russet potato slices have completely broken down (you can press them with a spoon or potato masher for an even thicker texture), the Yukon Golds are tender, the greens have softened, and the soup has thickened to a creamy consistency, 25 to 30 minutes (never really gets creamy)

Season soup to taste with salt and pepper and serve garnished with chives, if desired.
