Vietnamese Eggplant Recipe, Claypot Style From Beautiful Ninh Binh

Prep Time: 4 people 10 minutes Cook Time: 15 minutes Servings: 4 people Source: compassandfork.com

Ingredients

2 tsp peanut oil or coconut oil #

1 clove garlic #

2 tomatoes chopped

1 stem lemongrass pale, lower section, sliced thinly. Reserve stem #

1 1/2 tbsp fish sauce or soy sauce #

1 tsp palm sugar or raw sugar

1 onion, green (spring) white and green part, cut into 1" (2.5cm) lengths #

2 eggplants long-Asian style, cut into 1" X 1/3" (2.5cm X 1cm) pieces #

1 red chili, long Asian style seeds removed, cut into short, thin strips #

1 pinch turmeric powder curry powder can be substituted #

1 pinch black pepper, ground

Garnish

1 tbsp mint, Vietnamese finely chopped #

1 tbsp cilantro (coriander) finely chopped #

1 tbsp onions, green (spring) finely chopped #

1/2 tbsp red chilies, long Asian style sliced thinly #

Directions

Put a stovetop-safe, medium size clay pot* on medium heat. Add the peanut oil. When warm, add the garlic and stir gently until fragrant. Add the tomatoes, lemongrass and two tbsp water and cook for 2 minutes.

Add one tbsp of the fish sauce and the sugar. Mix well. Add the green onion and eggplants. Pour in a cup of water, add the remaining 1/2 tablespoon of fish sauce, mix well and bring to the boil.

Add the red chili, turmeric, ground pepper and lemongrass stalk. Allow mixture to simmer uncovered for 10 minutes or until eggplant is tender.in the claypot.

Whilst the eggplant is cooking, make the garnish by simply mixing all of the sliced ingredients together. Garnish ingredients

Remove the lemongrass stem and serve the eggplant mixture with steamed rice. Top with the garnish.

Notes

Don't despair if you don't have a clay pot! You can use a stovetop-safe casserole dish or a saucepan.

For lemongrass, only use the lower, pale portion of the stem (the first 3 or 4 inches) with the tough, outer leaves removed. Slice thinly across the stem. The upper part of the stem is great for flavoring stocks or for using as a skewer to cook meats. You can substitute with lemon peel.

You can substitute the 2 Asian-style eggplants with 1 egg-shaped eggplant.

This dish isn't overly spicy but if you dislike warm foods remove the seeds from the red chili in the garnish.
