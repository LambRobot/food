Pork Banh Mi

Slow cooking

Cook Time: 4 hrs

Ingredients

For The Meat


3-5# boneless pork shoulder roast

1/3C Lao Gan Ma Spicy Chili Crisp

5 cloves garlic, chopped

1.5 inch of ginger root, peeled and minced

2 whole star anise

1/4C fish sauce

1/4C soy sauce

1/2C Chinkiang black vinegar (you can sub 1 part red wine vinegar + 1 part balsamic vinegar)

1/4C Shaoxing rice wine or mirin

1-2 TBSP corn starch

For The Sandwiches


French loaves or bollilo rolls (I subbed King's Hawaiian rolls but be careful they get soggy fast)

mayonnaise

sriracha

reserved cooking liquid

english or persian cucumber, sliced into strips

jalapeno slices, as thin as possible (optional, omitted in picture)

carrot, julienned and quick pickled (1 part rice wine vinegar, 1 part water, 1 part sugar, soak at least 30m)

cilantro leaves

green onion, julienned

Directions

Sear the pork shoulder roast on all sides. Add all ingredients EXCEPT THE CORN STARCH to slow cooker and cook on HIGH for 3-4 hours until tender but not falling apart.

When pork is done, remove from cooking liquid and let cool.

While pork is cooling, add the corn starch and simmer the cooking liquid for about 10 minutes to reduce slightly. Remove the whole star anise. Pour liquid into a bowl and refrigerate. Once the cooking liquid has set, scrape off the excess fat and discard. Reserve sauce for assembly.

Once pork is cool enough to handle, slice across the grain into flat slices.

Assembly

Slice the rolls in half most of the way so the halves are still attached. At this point you have the option to lightly toast the bread in a toaster oven or broiler.

Thinly spread mayonnaise on each side with a drizzle of sriracha.

Add layer of cucumber, 1-2 slices of meat, a small spoonful of reserved cooking liquid, jalapeno slices, carrots (drained), cilantro, green onion, and top with another drizzle of sriracha. Enjoy immediately.
