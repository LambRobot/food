Here's my foolproof method:

1. ⁠Trim the fat. Look for any tough fat around the edges and trim the fatty side to like 1/4in. thick
2. ⁠Season and let it rest on the counter for 30ish minutes. Don't go overboard, but season it well enough where it's covered and even.
3. ⁠Set Traeger to 190 and slap that baby on there fat side down. Don't think about touching it until it stalls around 150-165, usually about 8 hours in.
4. ⁠Take off, wrap in butcher paper and put back on at 250.
5. ⁠Let it ride until at least 203 internal, but really you're looking for your probe to slide in like melted butter. This will probably take another 8-10 hours from when you wrapped and bumped the temp.
6. ⁠Remove from Traeger, keep it wrapped and cover it in towels and put it inside of a cooler for at least 2 hours. (NDLR dont need cooler) This is one of the more important parts because this is when the juices will redistribute and the fat will keep rendering.
7. ⁠Slice and enjoy!
