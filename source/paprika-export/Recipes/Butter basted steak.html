Butter basted steak

★★★★★

Prep Time: 35 mins Cook Time: 9 mins Difficulty: Medium Servings: 1 Source: Self

Ingredients

1 thick steak (ribeye is fatier and tastier, bone in even better, new york strip works make aure its thick enough)

1 garlic clove

1 branch of thyme

1 branch of rosemary

plenty of butter

Directions

30 Min prior, take the steaks out of the fridge and put them on a wooden board. Sprinkle them generously with coarse salt and grinded pepper, slap them before turning them over and repeat on the other side. Let them rest for 30min, liquid should be pooling out of the steak.

When ready for the great cookery, Prep all the herbs, ustensils (spoon, tong, meat thermometer), butter, etc. Thrust me, make sure everytbing is handy, coz its gonna be intense.

Pour a good film of vegetable oil in a cast iron pan. Ser the heat to the max and leave it there during the whole process.

Wait for the pan to be extremely hot, smoking smoking hot. Using the tong put the steak in the pan. It should scream sizzling and smoke. Move the steak around a bit, lifting it gently, making sure the underside is coated with the oil.

After 2-3 min, check if the steak is seared enough (should be able to make some sound if we were to tap it), and flip it over. After 2-3 min put a bit of butter on top of steak, rest of butter in the pan, with the farlic thyme and rosemary. Tilt the pan and using a spoon, baste the steak for a minute.

Check temperature of the steak (insert probe from the side). We want 125F. Keep basting until the temperature is reached, then remove the steak from the pan and let it rest 10min.

P.s.: the cast iron is now in a perfect state for sauteeing potatoes.

Dont overcrowd the pan
