Winter Tagine

Ingredients

The Protein


Chicken: 4–6 bone-in, skin-on thighs (or drumsticks), patted dry.


Seasoning: Salt, pepper, and 1 tbsp Ras el Hanout (for the chicken rub).


The Veggie Base (The Aromatics)


Onion: 1 large, diced.


Leek: 1 large, cleaned and sliced into rounds (white and light green parts).


Garlic: 3–4 cloves, minced.


Carrots & Celery: 3 carrots (sliced), 2 stalks celery (chopped).


The Hearty Vegetables


Butternut Squash: 1 small, peeled and cut into 1-inch cubes.


Delicata Squash: 1 medium, halved, seeded, and sliced into half-moons (keep the skin on).


Cauliflower: 1 small head, broken into florets.


The Pantry & Liquid


Beans: 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas or white beans, drained and rinsed.


Broth: 1.5 cups chicken or vegetable broth.


Spices: 2 tbsp Ras el Hanout (for the vegetable base).


Oil: 2 tbsp olive oil or avocado oil.


The Garnish (Optional Fresh Herb Pop)


Handful of fresh cilantro and parsley, finely chopped.


Zest and juice of 1 lemon.

Directions

Prep & Preheat: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) on the Convection Bake setting.

Sear the Chicken: Heat oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Season the chicken skin with salt, pepper, and a dusting of Ras el Hanout. Sear skin-side down for 6–8 minutes until deeply golden. Flip for 1 minute, then remove to a plate (it will finish in the oven).

Sauté the Aromatics: In the same pot, add the onions, leeks, carrots, and celery. Sauté for 5 minutes, scraping the bottom of the pot to release the browned chicken bits. Stir in the garlic and the remaining 2 tbsp of Ras el Hanout for 30 seconds until fragrant.

Assemble the Tagine: Add the butternut squash, delicata squash, and cauliflower florets. Toss well to coat in the spices. Pour in the broth and add the beans. Nestle the chicken pieces back on top of the vegetables, keeping the skin above the liquid line.

The Braise (Lid On): Cover the Dutch oven with its lid and place it in the center of the oven. Bake for 30 minutes. This steams the dense butternut squash and carrots until tender.

The Roast (Lid Off): Remove the lid. Continue cooking for another 15–20 minutes using the convection fan to reduce the sauce and caramelize the edges of the squash and chicken skin.

Rest & Serve: Remove from the oven. The squash should be fork-tender and the sauce slightly thickened. Sprinkle with the fresh herb/lemon garnish and serve over fluffed couscous.
