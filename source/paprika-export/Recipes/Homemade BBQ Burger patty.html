Homemade BBQ Burger patty

★★★★★

Prep Time: 5 mins Cook Time: 8 mins Difficulty: Easy Servings: 3

Ingredients

1 pack of grounded meat, divided in 200g portions (3)

3 slices of cheese

hamburger buns (hawain buns recommended)

burger seasoning

sauce

garniture (tomatoes, red cabbage, type of salad, advocado, onion, etc)

meat thermometer

Directions

Make sure to not over work the meat (the more the meat is knead, the denser the patty will be, you want to keep it not too dense).

Pre-heat the grill at max temp.

Divide the ground meat into 200g portions and form the patties. You want them somehwere around the size of your hand (slightly smaller). Compress and round off the edges.

Add seasoning on each side and slap it. Be generous with the seasoning.

Oil the grill grate with plenty of vegetable oil and add the patties on, set heat to med- high. Close the lid. After 3-4 min or when you have a good char and the meat is not falling appart, flip the burger.

Make sure to check internal temperature (and play with leaving lid open or closed), when reaching 120F+, add slice of cheese on top amd close lid. After 1-2 min or when cheese is melted, get the patties of the grill. You want 130F for medium rare and 140F for medium.

At high heat, toast the buns for 1-2 min (be careful, easy to burn them if you lose track of time).

When the bun is ready, set meat on bottom bun, spread (tartine) sauce on top bun, add garniture. Close. Devour.
